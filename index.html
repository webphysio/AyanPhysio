Types of treatments

Manual Therapy: Hands-on techniques applied by the physiotherapist to manipulate or mobilize joints, muscles, and soft tissues. This may include joint mobilization, soft tissue mobilization, and myofascial release.

Book Appointment

Exercise Therapy: Prescribed exercises and therapeutic movements designed to improve strength, flexibility, balance, coordination, and overall function. These exercises can be tailored to specific conditions or goals.

Book Appointment

Heat and Cold Therapy: The application of heat or cold to affected areas to alleviate pain, reduce swelling, promote circulation, and aid in the healing process. This may involve the use of hot packs, cold packs, or contrast therapy.

Book Appointment

Therapeutic Ultrasound: The use of high-frequency sound waves to generate heat and promote tissue healing. Ultrasound is commonly used for conditions like muscle strains, tendonitis, and joint stiffness. All these are treated here.

Book Appointment

Gait Training: Analysis and correction of abnormal walking patterns to improve mobility, balance, and coordination. This may involve exercises, assistive devices, or orthotic prescription.

Book Appointment

Respiratory Physiotherapy: Techniques to improve lung function, breathing patterns, and respiratory muscle strength. This is often used in conditions such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease

Book Appointment

Pain Management: Various strategies to manage pain, such as manual therapy, exercise, heat/cold therapy, electrotherapy, relaxation techniques, and education on pain management strategies.

Book Appointment

Education and Self-Management: Providing patients with information and resources to understand their condition, manage symptoms, and promote Home exercise programs &lifestyle modifications.

Workplace injuries can have a significant impact on both employees and businesses. "The Importance of Core Strengthening for Overall Health and Injury Prevention"

Core strengthening exercises play a vital role in maintaining overall health and preventing injuries. The core muscles, including the abdominal, back, and pelvic muscles, provide stability, support, and proper alignment for the entire body. In this blog post, we will explore the importance of core strengthening and how it can contribute to improved physical performance, injury prevention, and overall well-being. Enhanced Stability and Balance: A strong core provides a stable foundation for the body, improving balance and coordination. Core exercises engage the deep muscles that support the spine, helping to maintain proper posture and stability during various activities, such as walking, running, or lifting objects.
